Simfest 2022: Hobby Edition

Steam Simfest: Hobby Edition is a celebration of games that attempt to simulate a hobby or job. There are now hundreds of titles on Steam that do just this, and we're excited to feature them in our first Simfest this spring.

This event will feature 12 hours of original livestream programming, including talks and panels. Plus, developers can run their own livestreams and of course discount their games to get the most out of shining a light on this genre of games.

This even runs March 28 at 10am Pacific - April 4th at 10am Pacific.

Eligibility

The "Hobby Sim" subgenre is not yet well-defined within Steam, but this facet of Simulation gaming has become a big trend in recent months. As such, we have a set of criteria that we're using to define the parameters of this event, and inviting eligible games to participate.

A Hobby Sim title is one that attempts to simulate a hobby or job, where it's fun to try your hand at becoming an expert in the tasks at hand. To identify these titles, we've sought out those games which feature
  1. A first-person view of that hobby or job.
  2. A specific and intense gameplay focus on the accurate replication of the actions of that hobby or job.
  3. Digital reproduction of the equipment/machinery/objects used in that hobby or job.
  4. Gameplay elements such as narrative and progression which are deployed in a manner to enhance the replication of the hobby/job.
  5. Gameplay pacing which is generally set at a slower rate, consummate with the replication of the hobby/job.
  6. A game setting which is less about the imaginary/fantastical and more about a non-fictional activity. Where the job or hobby itself is fictional, the game is fastidious and authentic in portraying that hobby or job as though it were non-fiction.

Most titles that are invited to participate include all six of the above characteristics, though some may include just a majority of them, as they also fall within the spirit of the event.

For example, a simulation game like Dyson Sphere Program is amazing, but it's more of a base-building sci fi sim, as opposed to a digital recreation of a real-life job or task. This sale is targeted at the latter; games like Model Builder, PC Building Simulator, or PowerWash Simulator.

This event is also generally avoiding sports games, as we'll cover those titles in a separate event later in the year.

Invitation Process

We'll be inviting specific games to participate in Simfest: Hobby Edition based on the criteria above. This invitation will go out to the Steamworks users that have "Receive Steamworks Communication" permission for eligible games.

The invitation will ask you to register that game for Simfest, which is as easy as clicking one checkbox and saving. That registration will tell us which games intend to participate, and will unlock the ability for you to start scheduling your livestream sessions if you so wish.

Once registered, users in your account with "Edit Pricing Permissions" will then see Sim Fest appear in the new discounting approval tools and you can enter discounts for your game.

If you have not received an email invitation by March 9th and believe that you have a game that fits the criteria described above, please check your list of eligible games.

If your game is not present there and you believe it meets the above criteria, please Contact us here.

Discounts

A discount is not required to participate in Simfest, though the Simfest page will be prominently featuring discounted games. All invited titles can feature a discount unless you already have another discount scheduled too soon following Sim Fest.

Reminder: As of March 28th, all sales and discount opportunities are subject to the same 28-day cooldown between discounts. The only exceptions are the four major Steam seasonal sales. For more details on the discounting rule changes, please see Discounting Rule Changes

Once invited, visit your discounting dashboard (Apps & Packages -> Sale Approvals) to see your invited games and to enter any discounts.

Enter your discounts any time before the sale begins, on March 28th at 10am Pacific.

Livestreams

Livestreaming will also be a component of the Simfest event, with dedicated visibility given to games that are livestreaming. Here is how livestreaming will work for this event:
  • Once you have registered for Simfest, you can schedule up to two special Simfest Live Stream events between March 28th at 10am Pacific and April 1st at 10am Pacific. You'll find the new event type when creating a new Steam Announcement
  • These scheduled hour-long spots start on the top of each hour and will give your livestream a one-hour spot of higher visibility. When scheduling, please schedule your spot to begin on the hour (eg. "11:00", not "11:30") so you can get the most out of that spot.
  • From April 1st at 10am Pacific through the remainder of the event, we'll be hosting our own official livestream as the default livestream that visitors see. There are no scheduled spots available during this time for individual games, though you may continue livestreaming if you wish.

You may chose to run a livestream during parts of Simfest to show players what it's like to play your game.

Simfest Event Visibility

This is an official Steam Themed Sale and will be promoted significantly on the homepage of Steam and via our social media channels. The event will highlight games with discounts and will have a broadcast player showcasing games that are running livestreams.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q. Do I need to register my game before entering a discount?
A. Yes, you need to do the following things in order:
  1. follow the link in the invitation email, or click this link to register your game for Simfest. Or click this link to see your eligible games and register each https://partner.steamgames.com/optin/sale/simfest_2022/
  2. Once a game is registered, you can then enter discounts on the new Sale Approvals page here: https://partner.steamgames.com/promotion/discounts/dashboard/

Q. Do I need to schedule my livestream somewhere?
A. You may schedule up to two special events where you intend to be live with your game. You can do this once you have registered your game for Simfest. Simply go to post an announcement or event as usual under your registered game and you'll see a new category at the top for "Simfest Live Stream"

Note that you can only schedule a Simfest livestream to run between March 28th at 10am Pacific and April 1st at 9am Pacific. Starting on April 1 at 10am Pacific, the official livestream will be taking place of the livestream schedule.
